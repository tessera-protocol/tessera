# Internal Notes

- The public paper intentionally keeps “identity and authority for AI agents” as the top-layer framing for accessibility, while making capability-based security the actual technical backbone.
- The paper intentionally avoids over-indexing on OpenClaw, even though OpenClaw is the current strongest wedge, so the document does not read like a framework-specific memo.
- The semantic-enforcement limitation is stated directly on purpose. This is a trust-building choice, not a gap in editing.
- Federation remains in the roadmap, but only as an optional later phase. This is deliberate to avoid making the current product sound like a decentralised ecosystem thesis in search of a wedge.
- The role-template emphasis is stronger than in the prior draft because policy UX is now treated as the main adoption bottleneck, not a secondary product concern.
